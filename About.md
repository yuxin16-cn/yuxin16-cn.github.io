I work at the intersection of AI governance, cybersecurity regulation, and technical security evaluation for connected and software-driven products.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in BWL with a focus on Wirtschaftsinformatik from Freie Universität Berlin. After completing graduate-level coursework in Wirtschaftsinformatik, I started my career in global market access and regulatory intelligence, tracking international regulatory developments affecting connected products.

Fluent in English and German, with German academic and professional experience, I am able to analyze regulatory materials across jurisdictions, bridging the gap between official regulatory guidance and technical compliance implementation. This capability is particularly valuable in emerging regulatory landscapes for AI and IoT cybersecurity.

My current work includes:

• Cybersecurity evaluation and vulnerability assessment for IoT devices under ETSI EN 18031 (RED cybersecurity requirements), YY/T 1843-2022, GB/T 25000.51.
• Regulatory interpretation of frameworks such as the EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA), PSTI, Australia Cyber Security (Security Standards for Smart Devices) Rules 2025 and AI Act
• Preparation of technical and compliance reports for connected products and medical devices
• Development of internal compliance methodologies, SOPs, and evaluation frameworks
• Early-stage work exploring cybersecurity compliance consulting offerings for connected products
• Establishing technical communication channels, including company website and technical articles, while mitigating regulatory and copyright risks

I am particularly interested in aligning regulatory intent with practical evaluation methodologies, helping organizations navigate the intersection of AI governance, cybersecurity regulation, and product compliance.
